Insights and Observations

1. The analysis indicates that tsunami events were more frequent during the years 2013 and 2015.

2. The analysis shows that there was a slight rise in tsunami occurrences from 2013 to 2022.

3. The percentage of tsunami events increased from 2012 up to 2020 and continued to rise thereafter.

4. The distribution of earthquake magnitudes shows that most earthquakes had moderate magnitudes between 7.0 and 7.5

while a few major earthquakes were above 8.0 which were observed during the period.

5. The distribution of earthquake depths shows that most earthquakes occurred at moderate depths between 300 and 600 km,

while a few major earthquakes were observed at shallower depths between 0 and 200 km during the period.

6. The geographic distribution of earthquakes from 2001 to 2022 shows that tsunami occurrences varied by location.

7. Some regions experienced a higher number of tsunami events, while others had very few or none.

8. According to the tsunami highlights, certain locations experienced high-level tsunamis while others experienced low-level tsunamis.

9. Both high-level and low-level tsunamis were observed in roughly 25% to 40% of the cases.

10. he correlation analysis between seismic variables indicates that some variables, such as earthquake magnitude and depth, show a moderate positive correlation, suggesting that larger magnitude earthquakes tend to occur at certain depths.

Other variables, like latitude and tsunami occurrence, show little to no correlation, indicating that their relationship is weak or random.

11. The average magnitude of earthquakes that triggered tsunamis is higher compared to those that did not.

12. This indicates that stronger earthquakes are more likely to generate tsunamis, while lower-magnitude earthquakes generally do not result in tsunami events.
